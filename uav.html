Research Overview

Lean Burn Combustion for Aero-Engines

Our research group advances the science of combustion inside gas turbine engines used in aircraft and power generation. By focusing on lean burn technology, we reduce NOₓ emissions while maintaining stability across a wide range of fuels.

We combine state-of-the-art laser diagnostics, computational simulations, and novel fuel formulations to push the boundaries of what modern combustors can achieve.

Experimental Methods

Detailed Laser Diagnostic Experiments

We employ cutting-edge non-intrusive optical measurement techniques to characterize combustion and flow fields at micro-second timescales. Our lab houses high-power Nd:YAG laser systems, PCO high-speed cameras, and custom optical rigs.

  • PIV (Particle Image Velocimetry) — full-field velocity mapping of spray and flame interactions
  • PLIF (Planar Laser Induced Fluorescence) — species concentration and temperature measurement
  • Shadowgraph / Schlieren — high-speed visualization of shock waves and density gradients
  • Spray characterization — droplet sizing and atomization quality analysis
