Research Overview

Bipropellant Rocket Engine Research

Our group focuses on the fundamental and applied research of liquid bipropellant rocket engines. We design and study novel injectors — particularly pintle injectors — that offer superior mixing, throttling capability, and combustion stability for next-generation launch vehicles.

Combining experimental spray diagnostics with high-resolution CFD simulations, we characterize the complete combustion process from atomization to flame structure — targeting reusable, high-performance propulsion systems.

Injector Technology

Novel Pintle Injector Development

The pintle injector is a critical innovation for liquid rocket engines — used in engines like SpaceX's Merlin. Our group develops next-generation pintle designs with tangential fuel and oxidizer ports, optimized recess geometry, and enhanced impingement characteristics for superior atomization and mixing.

Fuel inlet + Oxidizer inlet with separate tangential ports for controlled impingement angle
Inner & Outer sleeves with recess geometry for enhanced propellant interaction zone
Throttleable design enabling deep thrust modulation — ideal for reusable rocket landing burns
Cold flow + hot fire testing with PIV and shadowgraph spray characterization

Application

Reusable Rocket Propulsion

Reusable launch vehicles demand engines capable of deep throttling — from full thrust at liftoff to low thrust during landing burns. The pintle injector's inherent throttling capability makes it ideal for this mission profile.

Our research directly addresses the combustion stability, heat transfer, and atomization challenges that arise at off-design throttle conditions — critical for reliable, repeatable reusability.
